Highlights & Recommendations

✓ Wins This Week

  • Total social sessions up +3.3% WoW (12,230 vs 11,839) and total users up +3.1% (9,532 vs 9,246) — steady acquisition growth heading into the beds selling season.
  • Organic Social bounce rate improved sharply: 0.61% vs 1.06% last week — a −42.5% improvement, meaning organic visitors are engaging more once they land.
  • Instagram saves up +80% WoW (36 vs 20) and content views up +32.1% (120,721 vs 91,406) — content is resonating even as some engagement metrics softened.
  • Facebook organic impressions up +10.5% and page visits up +4.6% — page-level discovery is trending the right way.
  • Paid Social bounce rate remains very low in absolute terms (0.65%), meaning traffic quality is still strong despite the relative WoW increase.

! Areas to Investigate

  • Organic Social revenue fell to £0 this week from £618 last week (−100%). Worth confirming e-commerce tracking is still firing correctly on organic social landing pages before assuming this is purely a content issue.
  • Paid Social revenue down −11.5% (£8,948 vs £10,112) while sessions were roughly flat (−4.8%) — revenue per session has softened. Check Meta AOV and product mix this week.
  • Total conversions down −6.5% WoW (19,353 vs 20,693), led by declines in both Paid Social (−6.9%) and Organic Social (−11.2%).
  • Pinterest impressions down −18.3% (84,593 vs 103,565) and outbound clicks down −21.8%. With beds season now underway (Jul–Aug), Pinterest content should be pivoting away from garden.
  • TikTok engaged audience down −55.7% and likes down −47.8%, despite video views growing +26.1% — reach is up but engagement depth is falling. Review hook and CTA strength in recent videos.
Next Steps — Prioritised
  1. Confirm Organic Social e-commerce tracking. Revenue dropped to £0 this week from £618. Check GA4 e-commerce events are still firing on landing pages reached via Instagram, Facebook and Pinterest links before concluding this is a content problem.
  2. Review Paid Social revenue efficiency. Sessions held roughly flat but revenue fell −11.5%. Pull Meta Ads Manager for 06–12 Jul and compare AOV and product mix against the prior week.
  3. Shift Pinterest content to beds. Impressions and outbound clicks both declined double digits. Garden season has passed its peak — schedule bed frame and bedroom styling pins per the seasonal calendar (Jul–Aug beds focus).
  4. Strengthen TikTok hooks and CTAs. Video views are up but engaged audience and likes are down sharply. Test stronger first-three-second hooks and clearer calls to action on upcoming uploads.
  5. Confirm July influencer activation. Monthly cadence requires at least one collaboration. If July's partnership isn't locked in, brief and book now with a focus on bedroom styling, and ensure all links are UTM-tagged for GA4 attribution.
