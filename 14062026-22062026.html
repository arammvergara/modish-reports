Highlights & Recommendations

Wins this week

  • Paid Social revenue up +10% (£8,588 vs £7,807) despite fewer sessions — traffic quality is improving, with higher revenue per session than the previous period.
  • Organic Social sessions up +9.4% and conversions up +17% — a positive signal that organic content is driving more engaged traffic. Worth doubling down.
  • Instagram website sessions up +42% (44 vs 31) — small absolute numbers but the strongest growth rate of any platform. The bio link or Stories CTA is working.
  • Pinterest sessions up +13.8% and conversions up +31.4% (941 vs 716) — Pinterest is outperforming its session share on conversion volume. High-intent audience.
  • Organic Social average session duration at 81.7s — over a minute on site vs 57s paid, suggesting organic visitors are more genuinely interested in the product.

Areas to investigate

  • Organic Social revenue dropped to £0 (prev: £826.50) — this is the biggest concern. Check whether UTM tagging on organic posts lapsed this week, or whether a high-converting piece of content stopped being served.
  • Paid Social sessions down −5.9% — review Meta campaign budgets and audience fatigue. With summer peak approaching, now is not the time to let paid reach slip.
  • Paid bounce rate up significantly (0.56% vs 0.24%) — while still very low in absolute terms, the doubling suggests landing page experience or ad-to-page relevance may have drifted. Check which ad sets are driving the most immediate exits.
  • TikTok delivers effectively zero website traffic (1 session) despite 5,500+ native video views. The link-in-bio and any swipe-up CTAs are not converting viewers to visitors. A TikTok Shop integration or stronger bio CTA would help close this gap.
Next Steps
1
Investigate organic revenue drop: Audit UTM parameters on all organic social posts from 14–22 Jun. If UTMs are missing or broken, sessions will have been miscategorised in GA4 (likely attributed to Direct or Other), masking the true revenue contribution. Fix tagging immediately to restore data integrity next week.
2
Review Meta campaign pacing: Paid sessions are down −5.9% heading into the seasonal peak (summer dining & outdoor furniture). Check campaign frequency, CPM trends, and audience overlap. Consider refreshing creatives if CTR has dropped — the bar stool and garden range perform well in summer.
3
Scale what's working on Pinterest: Pinterest is converting at a high rate relative to its session volume. Increase pin frequency on the dining and garden categories, and review which boards are driving the 510 sessions. A targeted Pinterest campaign layer could amplify this further at low CPM.
4
Fix the TikTok-to-website pipeline: 5,500+ TikTok video views with only 1 GA4 session is a critical gap. Update the bio link, add a direct product URL as a pinned comment on top videos, and consider TikTok Shop if not already live. Bridge the awareness-to-traffic gap before July.
5
July content brief: Begin briefing bed and bedroom content for the Jul–Aug seasonal peak. Organic Social is gaining momentum — feed it with high-quality lifestyle video and carousel content now to ensure the algorithm is primed before the peak period begins.
