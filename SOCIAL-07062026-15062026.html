Highlights & Recommendations

What's working

  • TikTok had a strong week across the board: total engagement +25.9%, likes +77.8% and reached audience +36.7% WoW. From a small base, but the direction is exactly right ahead of peak HomeToK discovery season.
  • Instagram profile views climbed +16.5% WoW (182,943 vs 156,993) — more people are landing on the Modish profile and taking a closer look, even as reach on individual posts cooled.
  • Facebook reach and page visits both improved (▲1.3% and ▲7.5% WoW), suggesting the page is being discovered by a slightly wider audience week on week.
  • Pinterest impressions rose +11.3% WoW (33,347 vs 29,968) — a positive trend as June sits at the tail of Modish's garden-content peak (Apr–May), where Pinterest planning behaviour is highest.

Where to focus

  • Instagram reach fell sharply (▼73.9% WoW, 1,823 vs 6,989) even as profile views rose — posts may be reaching fewer new accounts. Worth reviewing recent post formats, Reels frequency and posting times.
  • Pinterest engagements, pin clicks and saves all dipped 11–15% WoW despite higher impressions — content is being seen more but resonating less. Refresh pin creative with new garden-season imagery and boards.
  • Facebook's reported "Page followers" figure moved significantly between periods (9,786 vs 19,527)* — flag this with Meta Business Suite/Supermetrics before drawing conclusions on audience growth, as Meta reports this metric as a daily aggregate rather than a simple snapshot.
  • TikTok shares dropped from 9 to 2 (▼77.8% WoW) — identify which post drove last week's shares and test more of that format (transformations, before/afters, room reveals).
Next Steps
  1. Audit recent Instagram content (post types, Reels share, posting times, hashtags) to understand the reach drop and rebuild momentum — profile interest is rising, reach needs to follow.
  2. Lean further into TikTok — this week's engagement and reach gains suggest the current content direction is working. Plan 2–3 more room-styling or transformation videos to build on the momentum.
  3. Refresh Pinterest pin creative for the garden season tail (Apr–Jun peak) — new pins and updated boards to lift engagement and saves back up.
  4. Confirm Facebook/Meta follower reporting methodology directly in Meta Business Suite to establish a reliable week-on-week audience trend for the brand page.
  5. Progress this month's influencer collaboration — a styled-home feature from a UK interior creator could help offset softer Instagram reach with a fresh audience injection.
