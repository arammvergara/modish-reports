Highlights & Recommendations

What's working

  • Organic Social is the bright spot — sessions, users and new users are all up around 13% WoW, with conversions also ahead (+5%). Organic momentum is building nicely.
  • Pinterest had its strongest week yet: sessions, users and conversions all up 14–18% WoW. With her actively mood-boarding on Pinterest, this is exactly where Modish should be showing up.
  • Instagram conversions nearly doubled WoW (+83%), albeit from a small base — worth watching as IG content scales up.

Where to focus

  • Paid Social (Meta) revenue is down 42% WoW despite sessions holding fairly steady — a conversion efficiency issue rather than a traffic issue. Worth a closer look at campaign performance and landing pages.
  • Organic Social shows £0 attributed revenue this week, down from £1,096.50 — check GA4 e-commerce tracking and whether recent organic posts are linking through to shoppable pages.
  • TikTok activity is flat at zero this week — a gap given TikTok's strong discovery potential for the brand's target customer.
Next Steps
  1. Review Meta Paid Social campaigns for the week — check budget pacing, creative fatigue and landing pages on top-spending campaigns to address the 42% revenue drop.
  2. Investigate the £0 Organic Social revenue figure — confirm GA4 e-commerce/conversion tracking is firing correctly on links shared from Ilnstagram, Facebook and Pinterest posts.
  3. Lean into Pinterest with fresh seasonal pins (garden and dining imagery) to build on this week's strong growth in users and conversions.
  4. Plan a small TikTok content push — 2–3 short room styling or "what's new" videos — to start building presence ahead of peak HomeToK discovery season.
  5. Progress this month's influencer collaboration — confirm or brief at least one UK interior creator to feature a Modish piece in a styled home.
