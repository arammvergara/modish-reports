Highlights & Recommendations

✓ Wins This Week

  • Paid Social revenue more than doubled: £10,112 vs £4,442 (+127.7% WoW) — a standout week for paid social value, even as session volume dipped slightly.
  • Organic Social sessions and users both grew (+1.3% and +1.7%) — modest but genuine unpaid social growth.
  • Organic Social bounce rate improved slightly (1.06% vs 1.07%) — visitors from organic social are marginally more engaged.
  • Direct and Cross-network channels contributed strong secondary revenue: £6,037 and £5,732 respectively, reinforcing a healthy multi-channel mix.
  • Paid Search revenue held highest of all channels at £6,089 on modest volume (369 sessions) — efficient channel this week.

! Areas to Investigate

  • Blended social sessions, users, and conversions all declined WoW (−4.4%, −6.5%, −7.5%) despite the paid revenue spike — value per session is up but overall volume is softening.
  • Paid Social conversions down −7.0% (9,284 vs 9,979) alongside the revenue jump — worth confirming whether this is a genuine AOV increase or a small number of large orders skewing the total.
  • Organic Social conversions fell −12.6% (989 vs 1,131) and revenue dropped −37.3% (£618 vs £986) — the sharpest organic decline this week.
  • Unassigned channel bounce rate spiked to 23.58% — signals a UTM/attribution gap worth auditing, particularly around scheduled social posting tools.
  • Session duration remains untracked at channel-group level via the GA4 connector — continue monitoring engagement depth through the GA4 dashboard directly.
Next Steps — Prioritised
  1. Validate the Paid Social revenue spike. Cross-reference GA4 purchase_revenue against Meta Ads Manager for 29 Jun–5 Jul to confirm whether the +127.7% jump reflects genuine AOV growth or a handful of large orders before treating it as a repeatable trend.
  2. Investigate the blended social volume decline. Sessions, users, and conversions all fell WoW despite the revenue win. Review paid creative fatigue and organic posting cadence for the week to identify the cause.
  3. Audit organic social content from 29 Jun–5 Jul. Conversions dropped −12.6% and revenue −37.3%. Compare formats and CTAs against the stronger 22–28 Jun week and replicate what worked.
  4. Shift content toward beds & summer bedroom styling. July marks the start of Modish's bed-season focus (Jul–Aug). Prioritise bedroom and bed-frame content across paid and organic social now.
  5. Fix the Unassigned channel attribution gap. A 23.58% bounce rate suggests broken or missing UTM tags. Audit all scheduled social links (Later, Buffer, etc.) to ensure consistent utm_source/utm_medium tagging.
