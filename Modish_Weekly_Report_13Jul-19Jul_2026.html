Highlights & Recommendations

✓ Wins This Week

  • Total social sessions up +10.0% WoW (12,510 vs 11,375) — solid growth heading into late July.
  • Total users up +7.5% WoW (9,643 vs 8,967) — new visitor acquisition is tracking well across channels.
  • Total conversions up +2.5% WoW (20,259 vs 19,771), holding steady despite modest softness in individual social channels.
  • Paid Social bounce rate improved sharply: 0.52% vs 0.68% (−23.5%) — visitors arriving from paid social are more engaged than the week before.
  • Organic Social users edged up +0.5% even as sessions dipped slightly — an early signal of visitor quality holding up.

! Areas to Investigate

  • Paid Social revenue down −8.0% WoW (£8,232 vs £8,948) alongside a −4.4% dip in sessions — check AOV and product mix on Meta for the week.
  • Organic Social revenue sat at £0 for a second consecutive week despite 908 conversions — worth auditing UTM tagging and e-commerce event tracking on organic social links.
  • Organic Social bounce rate worsened: 0.80% vs 0.59% (+35.6%) — visitors from organic social are dropping off faster than last week.
  • Unassigned traffic carries an unusually high 88.66% bounce rate on 1,331 sessions — a meaningful volume of unclassified traffic worth investigating for tagging gaps.
  • Native platform activity softened this week (see native social report) — TikTok video views and Pinterest impressions both declined, which may be feeding the Organic Social dip here.
Next Steps — Prioritised
  1. Investigate the Paid Social revenue dip. Pull Meta Ads Manager for 13–19 Jul and compare AOV and product mix against the prior week to isolate the −8.0% drop.
  2. Fix Organic Social revenue attribution. Two straight weeks at £0 despite hundreds of conversions — audit UTM tagging on organic social links and confirm GA4 e-commerce tracking is firing correctly for these sessions.
  3. Audit Unassigned traffic tagging. 1,331 sessions and an 88.66% bounce rate is unusually high — check for missing UTM parameters or dark social traffic that should be reclassified.
  4. Address native platform softness. TikTok video views and Pinterest impressions both declined this week — refresh content cadence and align to the Jul–Aug bed season.
  5. Confirm July's influencer activation. Ensure at least one partnership is live this month with tagged links so its contribution to Paid/Organic Social can be measured in next week's report.
